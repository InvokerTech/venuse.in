The Space

A mix of modern architecture and old-world charm makes the Floating Farmhouse, an 1820 Catskills manor home featured by Dwell magazine and Architectural Digest, an inspired destination. Perched at the edge of a pristine creek and ancient dam of hand-laid stone, the home's grand spaces, dramatic details, and expansive aura are as enchanting as the landscape surrounding it. A cathedral-like kitchen features polished concrete floors, a wood-fired pizza oven, and a two-story wall of skyscraper glass framing views of the brook, gazebo, sweeping lawns, apple trees, and barn. French doors lead to a grand covered porch suspended over the water's edge, and the waterfall's cascading rhythms fill the cavernous great room and master suite. Original cast-iron beds and wavy glass windows adorn all sleeping quarters, and vintage farmhouse furnishings abound throughout. OCCUPANCY BREAKDOWN: Dinner party for 12 around kitchen table, for 24 if dining al fresco around the covered porch dining tables. 35 people for interior cocktail party, more if extended to outdoor living areas (such as covered porch). Up to 100 for weddings and receptions, lectures, and other outdoor events. ADDITIONAL FEES: Cleaning fee mandatory, on-site supervision fee and septic service fee/portable bathroom rental based on group size.

The Host

The homeowner is also the home's designer; it's one of several 19th century structures that Tom has renovated in a similarly historic/modern fashion. His projects have been featured by Dwell magazine, Architectural Digest, and other noteworthy design forums and publications. He was also featured as part of Porsche's "Powered by Design" video series, showcasing innovative artists and designers on the leading edge of their craft. Tom lives full-time in NYC but sneaks away to the country as often as he can.
